Important Dates in Women's History

July 1848

The first women's rights convention is held in New York, the Seneca Falls Convention.

May 1869

Susan B. Anthony and Elizabeth Cady Stanton establish the National Woman Suffrage Association.

April 1917

Jeanette Rankin is sworn in as the first woman elected to Congress as a Representative.

August 1920

The 19th Amendment is ratified, allowing women to vote.

December 1955

Rosa Parks refuses to give up her seat on the bus, setting the civil rights movement into action.

May 1960

The FDA approves the first birth control pill, allowing women the right to decide when they have children.

June 1963

President John F. Kennedy signs the Equal Pay Act.

July 1964

President Lyndon B. Johnson signs the Civil Rights Act.

June 1972

President Richard Nixon signs Title IX of the Education Amendments.

January 1973

Roe v. Wade; the Supreme Court declares the Constitution protects women's legal right to an abortion. It is overturned in 2022.

July 1981

Sandra Day O'Connor is sworn in as the first woman on the US Supreme Court.

September 1994

President Bill Clinton signs the Violence Against Women Act.

January 2007

Nancy Pelosi becomes the first woman to be a Speaker of the House.

January 2013

The US military removes the ban on women serving in combat positions.

January 2021

Kamala Harris becomes the first woman and first woman of color to serve as Vice President of the United States.
